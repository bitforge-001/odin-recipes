Pasta

Pasta is a traditional Italian dish made from unleavened dough of wheat flour mixed with water or eggs. It is typically formed into various shapes and sizes, such as spaghetti, penne, fusilli, and lasagna. Pasta is cooked by boiling it in water until it reaches the desired texture, usually al dente, which means it is firm to the bite. It can be served with a variety of sauces, including tomato-based sauces, creamy Alfredo, pesto, or simply with olive oil and garlic. Pasta is often garnished with cheese, herbs, and vegetables, making it a versatile and beloved dish worldwide.

Ingredients:

  • 200g of pasta (spaghetti, penne, etc.)
  • tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) of crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© for about 1 minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes to the skillet. Stir well and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat it evenly with the sauce. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh basil or parsley. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious pasta!
